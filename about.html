About Me

I am Imani Glasco, a Digital Media Innovation student focused on building a foundation in UX/UI design, web development, and digital communication. I am still exploring where I want to specialize, but I do know that I am drawn to work that combines thoughtful design with real usability. I am especially interested in how people interact with digital spaces and how small design decisions can shape that experience.

I came into this field without much of a design background. Most of what I have learned has come from building, testing, and figuring things out as I go. That process has pushed me to pay closer attention to how and why design choices work. I enjoy experimenting across different types of projects, from web layouts to content strategy. Each project has helped me understand how design, structure, and communication connect. It has also helped me develop a stronger sense of detail and consistency in my work.

One of my biggest strengths is staying calm under pressure. In stressful situations, I can step back, process what is happening, and make clear decisions. That carries over into my work when I am solving problems or working through challenges. I am also highly personable and collaborative, and I value direct, clear communication when working with others. I tend to work best in environments where I can engage with people, ask questions, and contribute to a shared goal.

I am still learning the nuances of design, including layout, typography, hierarchy, and spacing. Building that foundation is a priority, and I am focused on understanding the reasoning behind those systems, not just applying them. I want my work to be grounded in strong design principles rather than guesswork. I am alwaysgr looking to continue building my skills through hands-on projects while refining my direction within UX/UI. I am especially interested in opportunities that allow me to learn, experiment, and contribute to thoughtful, well-designed digital experiences. I am motivated by growth and by the chance to keep improving through real-world work.
